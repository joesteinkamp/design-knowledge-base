# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Working product designers, design-curious designers (PMs, engineers leaning in), and design students. They arrive wanting to become better at the craft: looking for a tool, a reference, a screenshot, a perspective, or a mental model. State of mind is exploratory and self-directed, not task-bound. They came to learn or to grab something useful, then leave.

## Product Purpose

A public, open-source launchpad that routes visitors to apps, tools, and documentation that help them grow as designers. The site itself is not the destination; it surfaces the things that are. Success is when someone leaves with a tool installed, a doc bookmarked, or a new mental model, and comes back next time they need one.

## Brand Personality

Curious, experimental, academic.

Voice is thoughtful and direct. Comfortable with rough edges and works-in-progress. Treats design as a discipline worth studying, not a vibe. Confident enough to publish unfinished things, humble enough to call them unfinished. Not corporate, not playful-startup, not influencer.

## Anti-references

- Polished SaaS landing pages with gradient hero metrics, identical feature card grids, and "trusted by" logo bars. This is not a product pitch.
- Influencer-design portfolios that center the author's persona over the work. The work is the point.
- Generic "design system" sites that look indistinguishable from each other (white background, teal accent, Inter, big rounded cards).
- Over-designed brutalism for its own sake. The academic register is genuine, not performative.

## Design Principles

1. **Everyone should be a better designer.** Every choice on the site should answer "does this help someone grow?" If not, cut it.
2. **The site is an exhibit.** Practice what you preach. A design knowledge base whose own design is generic undermines its premise.
3. **Routing over retention.** This is a launchpad. Get people to the right destination quickly. Do not invent reasons to keep them here.
4. **Show works in progress.** TBD cards, drafts, and half-built tools are welcome. The honesty is part of the brand.
5. **Earn every element.** Cards, sections, and copy each justify their space. No filler, no decorative chrome.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Default-good. WCAG AA contrast, keyboard-navigable, sensible focus states, respects `prefers-reduced-motion`. No specific known user needs beyond standard inclusive defaults.
